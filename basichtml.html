Wayne Gretzky Life

Wayne Gretzky, born 1961 in Brantford, Ontario. Known as the greatest of all time, or the "Great One." Gretzky dominated the NHL and holds many records to this date.

Early Life

Gretzky started skating at the age of two and was already playing on an organized club team by the age of six. By the late 1970s he began playing on a junior team called the Indianapolis Racers in the WHA, then was quickly drafted to the Edmonton Oilers in 1979.

The Great One's Historic Career

During his 20 seasons in the NHL, Wayne Gretzky played for the Edmonton Oilers, Los Angeles Kings, St. Louis Blues, and New York Rangers. He won four Stanley Cups with the Oilers and nine Hart Trophies as the league’s MVP, retiring while holding 61 NHL records.

Highlights of Career

  1. 4-time Stanley Cup Champion (1984, 1985, 1987, 1988)
  2. 9-time NHL MVP (Hart Trophy)
  3. 10-time NHL scoring leader
  4. 61 NHL records held at retirement
  5. Inducted into the Hockey Hall of Fame in 1999

Influences

  • “The Great One” nickname
  • Transformed hockey in the United States
  • Role model for hockey worldwide
