Life About Wayne Gretzky

Wayne Gretzky, born 1961 in Brantford, Ontario. Known as the greatest of all time or also known as the "Great One". Gretzky dominated the NHL holding many records to this date.

Early Life

Gretzky started skating at the age of two and was already playing on an organized club team by the age of six. By the late 1970's he began playing on a junior team called the Indianapolis Racers in the WHL, then was quickly drafted to the Edmonton Oilers in 1979.

The Great One's Historic Career

During his 20 seasons in the NHL, Wayne Gretzky played for the Edmonton Oilers, Los Angeles Kings, St. Louis Blues, and New York Rangers. He won 4 Stanley Cups with the Oilers and won 9 Hart Trophies being the league’s MVP, then retired holding 61 records in the NHL.

Highlights of Career

  1. 4-time Stanley Cup Champion (1984, 1985, 1987, 1988)
  2. 9-time NHL MVP (Hart Trophy)
  3. 10-time NHL scoring leader
  4. 61 NHL records held at retirement
  5. Inducted into the Hockey Hall of Fame in 1999

Influences

  • “The Great One” nickname
  • Transformed hockey in the United States
  • Role model for hockey worldwide
